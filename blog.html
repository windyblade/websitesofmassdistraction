Management of absence issues can be as difficult or easy as you make it.

Absence can hit a business hard whether it is short-term or long-term. If short-term, then typically the absence is reported with little to no notice which can make covering that work difficult due to very little time to plan. Whereas long-term absence can also be difficult for various other reasons, including not knowing whether the employee will actually be fit again to return to their job.

Sickness absence can also difficult in the sense that it is tough for people managers and/or business owners to legitimately assess whether an employee is truly ill and therefore incapable of turning up at work or not. Whilst there are steps that can be taken to help manage such situations, a great starting point is to build a foundation of trust and to create a working environment and relationship with all employees whereby you know your workers will come into work if they really can. Thereafter you can manage sickness issues for what they really appear to be, ill-health.

Now there are many different ways you can manage absence levels ranging from a trigger-scoring system to a frank and honest conversation with the employee about the concerns you may have. To help identify which course of action and support is required with any particular situation, HRA will assess what has been done and tailor a specific approach and set of recommendations for your business that works for you.

Whilst many companies have systems in place that help to record and keep a track of absence dates and number of occurrences etc, HRA can help support businesses understand what trigger points you need to look out for and what truly is an unacceptable level of absence, understanding that every situation is different and every businesses need for attendance varies too.

A formal discussion with the employee following a period of absence, whether that is short or long-term, is vital to ensuring the successful management of any absence. It helps protect the position of both the employee and employer for different reasons. For the employee to continue feeling motivated and driven to perform in their role, they need to feel that the company understands their reason for the absence and that the company will provide any required support that may be needed to help limit any further absence moving forwards. For the business a written log of the reasons and dates relating to the absence is key for ensuring it is managed accordingly, especially given a record of the discussions will be needed if any formal action is taken at any point. For assistance on how those discussions can be conducted, HRA can provide support and guidance and help businesses to understand what type of information is important to get out of the meeting.
